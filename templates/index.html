About

HamTTPD is an online service that can be used over the amateur radio service. To comply with regulations, communications are not encrypted. Of course, no one should send password over an unencrypted channel. But it is still possible to authenticate, thanks to a little-known feature of HTTPS: client certificates.

Currently, HamTTPD only accept certificates from LotW. However, if local authorities start issuing certificates, they could be supported as well. Ideally, the IARU would be the root certificate authority, delegating to local authorities (FCC, MIC, ANFR, OFCOM, BNeztA, â€¦). Each authority would then produce certificates for individual amateur radio operators. These final certificates could have a short duration (e.g. 3 months) and be renewed automatically.
